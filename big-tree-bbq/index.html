One family. One tree. One pit.

The big tree that fed the East End

After 28 years at the Cayman Islands Health Services Authority, Henry Harris retired at 60 — and lit a barbecue in his own front yard.

What began as a retirement hobby became an East End institution. Set under a huge shade tree in Gun Bay, Henry and his son Arvin have been cooking beef ribs, chicken and real Caymanian dishes for locals and travellers ever since — the kind of slow, honest food you can’t rush and can’t fake.

There’s no dining room and no gimmicks. Just charcoal, a family that loves to feed people, and the best seats on the island: under the branches, out in the open, with the trade winds coming off the sea.

— Henry & Arvin Harris
