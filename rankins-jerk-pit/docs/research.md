## Company background

- Run by **Miss Rankin**, who learned to cook from her mother (Cayman Good Taste, Fodor's).
- **Sister business:** Rankin's butcher shop and farm adjacent to the restaurant,
  supplying "ultrafresh produce and meats" (Fodor's).
- Setting: roadside garden just before Bodden Town (heading east on Shamrock Road, past
  Bodden Town school). Landmarks: picnic tables, a garden where chickens wander at your
  feet, and **life-sized faux livestock — a cow and a pig — greeting visitors at the
  entrance** (Fodor's, CaymanVisitor). Order at a window, then eat in the garden or take away.
- Single location. No franchise or co-op ties found.
- Press/guide mentions: Fodor's Travel review, Cayman Good Taste "Best Local Caribbean
  Restaurants in Grand Cayman", CaymanVisitor dining guide, Red Sail Sports blog,
  FED Guides "Best Jerk Chicken in Cayman" video feature, Wanderlog "best jerk chicken"
  lists for Grand Cayman and the Cayman Islands.

4. **"Follow The Line of Locals"** — Tripadvisor reviewer: the line of locals tells you

**Recurring praise:** best jerk on the island; real charcoal fire; huge cheap portions;
authenticity; friendly staff; the garden.
**Recurring complaints (for the site to fix):** jerk chicken sells out in the evening and
menu items go unavailable — visitors show up late and leave empty-handed; a few find it
overrated when they arrive after sell-out. → The site must set expectations: show hours
prominently, push "come early / call ahead" and click-to-call.

## Competitors (Grand Cayman jerk / local Caribbean)

- **Chicken! Chicken!** (Seven Mile Beach) — strongest digital player: award-winning,
  wood-roast concept, real website with delivery/online ordering. Polished but franchise-
  clean; nothing rustic or personal.
- **Peppers Bar & Grill** (SMB) — casual tourist bar & grill; jerk is one item among
  ribs/steak/pizza. Generic glossy site.
- **Red Bay Jerk Chicken** (Red Bay roadside stand) — beloved drum-grill stand; effectively
  no web presence.
- **Sam's Quality Jerk & More** — well-reviewed food, run-down premises, no real web presence.
- **Takeaway:** among the authentic roadside jerk spots, nobody has a real website. A fast,
  honest site with hours, menu and click-to-call instantly makes Rankin's the most findable
  authentic jerk spot on the island. E-commerce/booking is not the local pattern — phone
  and walk-up is.

## Market context

- Shamrock Road is the main artery east: everyone driving to Bodden Town beaches, East End
  and Rum Point passes the door — drive-by discovery matters; the site's job is pre-trip
  confirmation (open now? what's cooking?).
- Customer mix: locals are the weekday core (the "line of locals"); stay-over tourists and
  cruise-day drivers add volume, peaking in high season (Nov–Apr).
- Supply is genuinely local (own farm/butcher) — import factors matter less than for most
  Cayman restaurants; sell-outs are the constraint, not shipping.
- Seasonality: evening sell-outs already happen; high season amplifies them. Site should
  encourage early visits and Saturday BBQ awareness.
