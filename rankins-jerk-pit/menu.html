The Menu

Cooked slow over real coals.

Plate food, not plated food. Everything below is cooked in the yard on Shamrock Road — and when it's gone, it's gone.

From the Pit

Off the coals

The drums get lit early. Charcoal only — that's the flavor people drive across the island for.

  • Jerk Chicken

    Marinated overnight, grilled slow on the drum, served with our hot jerk sauce on the side. The one the reviews are about.

  • Jerk Pork

    Hours over the coals until it pulls apart. Ask for extra sauce if you think you can handle it.

Cayman Kitchen

The old dishes

The recipes Miss Rankin learned from her mother. This is the food Cayman grew up on — most days, most of it; every day, some of it.

  • Turtle Stew

    The signature. Old Cayman cooking, done slow the traditional way — from Cayman-farmed turtle.

  • Oxtail

    Braised until it falls off the bone, in a rich gravy over rice and beans.

  • Curry Goat

    Proper West Indian curry — deep, warm heat, big portions.

  • Lobster Curry

    When the season allows. Ask at the window or call ahead.

  • Stew Beef

    Slow-stewed with meat from our own butcher next door.

  • Soup of the Day

    Whatever the kitchen's simmering — ask what's in the pot.
